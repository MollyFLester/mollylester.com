HUBBLE

Hubble is a conceptual online learning platform that teaches coding through the creation of an intellectual community. As part of the exploratory project, my team was tasked with creating a better online learning experience using the design thinking approach. The website development process took us through research, synthesizing, wireframing, testing and finally the creation of UI.

EMPATHIZE & DEFINE

QUALITATIVE DATA

Using the agile methodology, my team started by creating a research plan to help define how we are going to attack the problem. Together we collected extensive quantitative data on not only on how people use online learning, but why they were using the online medium and what pain points they had throughout the experience.

Key Takeaways

1.

Confused about their progress within the course

2.

Felt alone in the learning process, not only from lack of social interaction, but also when it came to finding help

3.

Bored and didn’t feel like an active participant in their learning

SYNTHESIS

AFFINITY DIAGRAMMING

Taking the raw data, and key takeaways, we began to see patterns in the research and focus our scope to just online coding schools. Distilling that information into more actionable forms we created: a problem statement, design principles, and personas.

The user wants increased human connection to further the interactivity of their lessons. This will keep interest, improve retention of material, and feel like their learning is more personalized.

DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Empower the User

Create an atmosphere where the student feels as if they are creating the answers, and empower them to choose how they learn.

Progress is Everything

Give the user assurance that they are moving forward with every task they complete.

Everything Should Lead to Real Life

The end result must be something they can use in their continuing careers.

Don't Overwhelm the User

Break down all information into digestible chunks that build upon each other.

Humanity is Key

The user may be doing the lesson on their own, but they should not feel alone in the overall experience.

Be Forgiving

Reassure the user that every mistake is ok, and redirect them to the correct path.

Delightful at Every Step

Infinitesimal pieces of interaction compound into a joyful personality and experience for your users.

Dig In, Not Ouw

Information should be built in on deeper levels. It should all be easily accessible to the user if they would like, but is hidden so it doesn’t overwhelm.
